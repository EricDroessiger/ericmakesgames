Description

Bioside was the final project in my 2nd Year of University.

The game received an award for the best Visuals within the 2nd year projects.

Bioside is a VR game combining shooting and Zero-G movement into an action-packed experience. The main goal was to create innovative movement in virtual reality while merging elements of the shooter genre. Therefore, we took inspiration from other VR games like Lone Echo and Robo Recall, to create interesting movement, while still delivering action-packed combat.

My Contribution

During the project I was working as a Scrum Master and UI/UX Designer. I was also involved in creating the original concept and pitching it within the team.

As a UI/UX Designer, I researched and defined UI with Motion Sickness in mind. Also gained experience on how to implement and balance Haptic Feedback.

  • Organize the daily standups
  • Sprint Planning
  • Managing tasks in Jira
  • Research UI and motion Sickness in VR
  • implement UI/UX elements: Weapon Holsters, Bloody Screen & haptic feedback for Weapons
  • Initial Movement Balance to prevent Motion Sickness

All concepts we brainstormed and pitched within the team.

Challenges

Scrum Process

I found it very challenging to consistently maintain the Scrum process since the whole team was new to the tool and needed support in understanding the tool. In addition, this was my first time working with 18 other people in a team. Depending on the team next Year, we might want to simplify the Scrum Process and use weekly Sprints instead of fortnightly sprints to iterate on the Project and Production Process itself.

3D UI

A special challenge was to create 3D UI elements in UE4 since the UI tools are only supporting transparency in 2D. To create a simple Bloody Screen one of the Artists had to create a Sphere with a hole that is placed in front of the camera.

Lessons Learned

What to keep doing?

  • Considering the amount of time, we managed to create a small but cool experience for the player. The enthusiasm kept us going, wanting to improve the game, while we made sure to keep a good work balance.
  • It was a good idea to have one dedicated computer for Testing VR. In this way, every developer had the chance to test their work. of course, more test stations or VR Headsets would have been better.
  • Playing and researching other VR Games or "experiences" helped me experiencing Motion Sickness myself and keep a good eye on it when balancing the player movement for the first demonstration.

What to do better next time?

  • Sometimes I need to start early and just create something. I sometimes tend to overthink tasks which turn out to be faster to achieve than I thought. By improving this I can get feedback faster and more often, improving my work more.
  • As Scrum Master, I learned that I should never focus too much on recommended practices and give the principles of Agile Development the highest priority.
  • The game could still be improved by making sure the Mechanics interact more with each other to create ways for the player to combine the different tools emergently in the environment.
